<html><body bgcolor="#ffffff" text="#000000"><table><tr><td colspan="2"><h3>Problem Statement</h3></td></tr><tr><td>&#160;&#160;&#160;&#160;</td><td><p>
Roger the Robot has been sent to explore a planet. 
The surface of the planet can be thought of as a two-dimensional plane. 
You are given two vector &lt;int&gt;s <b>x</b> and <b>y</b>.
The planet has N interesting points described by these vector &lt;int&gt;s.
The i-th interesting point has coordinates (<b>x</b>[i], <b>y</b>[i]).
No three interesting points will be collinear.
</p>

<p>
Roger will choose a permutation of {0,1,...,N-1}, and will visit the points in that order.
Roger will travel in a straight line in between points. There are two conditions he must follow:
<ol>
<li>He must never cross his own path (that is, if we look at the line segments formed by the path, no two segments strictly intersect). </li>
<li> Due to rather unfortunate oversight, Roger is incapable of making any right turns. This means that for any three consecutive points that he visits, these three points constitute a counter-clockwise orientation. </li>
</ol>
</p>

<p>
Your job is to find a path that Roger can take.
If there is no valid path, return an empty vector &lt;int&gt;.
Otherwise, return an vector &lt;int&gt; containing a permutation of 0,...,N-1, representing a valid path that Roger can take.

</p>
